## Finding 3: "Sell the News" Phenomenon Exists

**Definition:**  
Stock drops despite beating earnings estimates (expectations were too high).

**Frequency:** 8 out of 68 events (11.8%)

**Examples:**

GOOGL, Apr 2024: Beat by +6.2%, Stock fell -3.2% 
AMZN, Jul 2023: Beat by +8.5%, Stock fell -2.9% 
TSLA, Jul 2024: Beat by +4.8%, Stock fell -2.4%


**Why This Happens:**
1. Stock ran up before earnings (priced in)
2. Beat wasn't "big enough" for high expectations
3. Weak revenue or guidance overshadowed EPS beat
4. Profit-taking by traders

**Lesson:** Beating earnings doesn't guarantee gains!

**Key Finding:**  
> **Contrarian signal:** Stocks that fell before earnings often bounce back afterward (+1.20% avg). Stocks that rallied hard before often disappoint (-0.85% avg).

**Strategy Implication:**  
- Avoid "hot" stocks going into earnings (ran up too much)
- Consider "beaten down" stocks (potential relief rally)

## Conclusion

**Main Takeaway:**
> Earnings announcements create short-term volatility but have **small average impact (+0.43%)**. Beating estimates improves odds (71% win rate) but doesn't guarantee success.  **Microsoft** is the most reliable earnings play, while **Google** consistently disappoints.

**For Long-Term Investors:**
- Ignore short-term earnings volatility
- Focus on fundamentals over multiple quarters
- Don't panic-sell on earnings misses

**For Active Traders:**
- Only trade earnings with edge (e.g., MSFT history, contrarian plays)
- Use small position sizes (high volatility)
- Set strict stop-losses
- Recognize this is gambling, not investing
